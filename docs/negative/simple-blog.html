Rebuilding My Workshop: Lessons in Woodworking

Published on February 18, 2026 · 6 min read

After years of cramming tools into a single-car garage, I finally took the plunge and built a dedicated workshop in the backyard. The process took three months, a lot of lumber, and more trips to the hardware store than I can count.

The biggest lesson was in planning. I spent two full weeks sketching layouts on graph paper before cutting a single board. Where does the table saw go relative to the dust collector? How much clearance does the bandsaw need? These questions seem obvious in hindsight, but getting them wrong would have meant tearing everything out and starting over.

I opted for a French cleat system on the walls, which lets me rearrange tool holders without drilling new holes. The electrical plan was another beast entirely — I ran a dedicated 60-amp sub-panel with 20-amp circuits for each major tool. No more tripping breakers mid-cut.

The dust collection system deserves its own post (coming soon). For now, I will just say that a 4-inch trunk line with blast gates at each station has been a game changer. My lungs thank me.

Five Underrated National Parks for Spring Hiking

Published on February 3, 2026 · 4 min read

Everyone flocks to Yosemite and Yellowstone, but some of the best spring hiking happens in lesser-known parks. Congaree in South Carolina offers boardwalk trails through old-growth floodplain forest, and in April the fireflies put on a show that rivals anything you have seen.

Great Basin in Nevada is another hidden gem. Lehman Caves alone are worth the drive, but the alpine trails above 10,000 feet offer solitude that is hard to find elsewhere. Just bring layers — temperatures can swing 40 degrees in a single day.
